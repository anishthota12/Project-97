What is Climate Change?

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is the way that the Earth heats up. When solar radiation reaches the atmosphere, a part of the radiation is re-radiated by greenhouse gases. The energy heats the Earth's surface and the atmosphere.

The Greenhouse Gases

• Carbon Dioxide (CO 2)
• Methane (CH 4)
• Nitrous Oxide (N 2 O)
• Water Vapor (H 2 O)

Causes Section

How the Climate is Warming

Burning Fossil Fuels

We burn fossil fuels for fuel. Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide, which is a greenhouse gas.


Livestock Farming

Livestock farming affects climate change because livestock such as cattle, pigs, sheep, and goats emit carbon dioxide and methane, both greenhouse gases.

Deforestation

Deforestation is the removal of trees from the land. However, trees take in carbon dioxide from the air and releases oxygen. As a result, more carbon dioxide is in the air.

Solar Radiation

Solar radiation adds more heat to the Earth, especially to the poles. This causes ice sheets to melt and adds to global warming.


Overpopulation

Overpopulation means more people. More people means more demand for oil and fuel, which means more carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.





Effects Section

What Will Happen to Earth


Melting Ice/Higher Sea Levels

The warming climate will make ice sheets in Antarctica and Greenland will melt, causing the oceans to have higher sea levels and sending floods in coastal cities.

Warming Oceans

Climate change will cause the oceans to warm, affecting the creatures of the ocean ecosystems.

More Extreme Storms

The warming of the Earth is going to make more moisture in the air, meaning more rainfall. Droughts, heat waves, and hurricanes will also grow in strength and intensity.

Harder Farming

The climate will cause higher temperatures, eventually reducing yields of crops. It will also help weeds and pests.


Affecting People's Health

Climate change will increase the chances of breathing and heart diseases, as well as premature deaths.
